Round 4 at Circuit Zolder
**for immediate release**
May 15, 2024

Race Report: GT3 World Championship - Round 4 at Circuit Zolder

  • Location: Heusden-Zolder, Limburg, Belgium
  • Track: Circuit Zolder – Alternate
  • Weather: Clear skies, 78°F, light wind from the north at 2 mph
  • Date: May 15, 2024
  • Race Distance: 31 laps

Qualifying Recap

In qualifying, Jens Braun (#10) stole the show with a blazing lap of 86.31 seconds, putting him on pole. Jason Davison (#66), close behind with a time of 86.46 seconds, secured second, followed by Tobias Steiner (#33) and Thierry Bernard (#911), who rounded out the top four. The tight margins set the stage for a thrilling race as the top contenders were mere tenths of a second apart.

Race Recap: Davison’s Dominance and the Battle Behind

From the start, Jason Davison capitalized on his front-row position, taking the lead early and maintaining control through the majority of the race. Known for his aggressive driving style, Davison skillfully defended his lead against repeated challenges, particularly from Jens Braun, who briefly took the lead for five laps. However, Davison reclaimed his position, showcasing his resilience and precision on Zolder’s unforgiving track. Pierre Dupont (#130) and Lukas Huber (#44) also demonstrated impressive pace, with Dupont moving up from his fifth-place start to battle among the top three. Huber, starting from the eighth position, fought his way through the field, finishing a commendable fourth and maintaining his consistency in the points standings.

“Zolder doesn’t forgive mistakes. I knew I had to stay focused, especially with Braun pressuring me,” Davison commented after the race. “This win feels well-earned.”

Midfield Action and Strategic Overtakes

The middle of the pack saw intense battles for position, especially among drivers like Pascal Lefevre (#6), who started ninth, and Markus Weber (#3), who showed grit in defending his spot. Thierry Bernard (#911) struggled to break through the top three despite his fourth-place start, eventually finishing fifth. As the laps dwindled, the technical demands of Zolder required precise tire and fuel management, with some drivers conserving just enough to make bold moves in the final laps.

“It was all about timing and precision today,” Dupont shared. “The team strategy was perfect, and I managed to make those moves stick when it mattered most.”

Finishing Order

  1. Jason Davison (#66) - 31 laps, 200 points
  2. Jens Braun (#10) - +9.36 seconds, 180 points
  3. Pierre Dupont (#130) - +10.70 seconds, 160 points
  4. Lukas Huber (#44) - +18.75 seconds, 140 points
  5. Thierry Bernard (#911) - +18.89 seconds, 120 points

Final Thoughts

Circuit Zolder once again proved to be a track where precision and patience are paramount. With no cautions throughout the race, drivers were pushed to their limits, knowing that every small error could mean the difference between a podium finish and missing out on points. Jason Davison’s commanding performance reestablishes him as a formidable competitor this season, while Jens Braun’s consistent showing keeps him in the mix for the championship. As the series heads into the next round, drivers are aware that every point counts in this increasingly competitive season.
