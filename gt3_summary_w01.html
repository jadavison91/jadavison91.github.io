Race Report: GT3 World Championship - Round 1 at Canadian Tire Motorsports Park

Race Summary

The opening round of the 2024 IMSA GT3 World Championship at the iconic Canadian Tire Motorsports Park delivered an electrifying start to the season. With its dramatic elevation changes and notoriously tricky corners like the Moss Corner hairpin, the 2.459-mile circuit set the stage for an intense battle among 33 world-class drivers, each vying for victory. Qualifying Drama Sets the Tone Jason Davison (#66) stormed to pole position with an impressive lap of 1:17.27, edging out Thierry Bernard (#911) by a fraction of a second. Bernard’s close second-place qualifying time, just 0.13 seconds behind, set up an intense showdown, with Lukas Huber (#44) and Tobias Steiner (#33) securing third and fourth positions. Davison’s pole hinted at a strong showing, but as the race unfolded, the battle for victory proved anything but predictable. Race Recap: Bernard’s Resilience Pays Off From the start, Davison capitalized on his pole position, taking the early lead with Bernard in close pursuit. Despite his rapid start, Davison faced relentless pressure, especially as the track’s high-speed twists tested the drivers’ grip and nerve. Bernard and Davison exchanged the lead several times as they navigated the demanding layout, with neither driver willing to give an inch. The action hit a fever pitch around lap 20, as Bernard finally broke through, capitalizing on a minor slip by Davison out of the Mario Andretti Straight. Bernard took the lead and maintained it, displaying exceptional composure in the final laps to secure his victory. > 'Mosport is unforgiving. You can’t afford to blink,' Bernard remarked post-race. 'The pressure was on all the way.' Huber and Lefevre Show Grit Meanwhile, Huber and Pascal Lefevre (#6) put on a fantastic show of grit, fighting through the middle pack to claim second and third places, respectively. Their duel intensified on lap 27, with Huber defending his position aggressively through Moss Corner. Lefevre managed to slip by on the Andretti Straight but couldn’t close the gap to Bernard before the checkered flag. > 'I gave it everything through those last few laps,' Lefevre shared. 'Kevin [Bernard] was just untouchable today.'

Finishing Order

1. Thierry Bernard (#911) - 31 laps, 200 points 2. Lukas Huber (#44) - +0.93 seconds, 180 points 3. Pascal Lefevre (#6) - +2.47 seconds, 160 points 4. Jason Davison (#66) - +9.43 seconds, 140 points 5. Tobias Steiner (#33) - +9.19 seconds, 120 points

Final Thoughts

With three lead changes and zero cautions, this opening race at Mosport has set a high bar for the season. The drivers have shown their mettle, and the championship battle is already heating up as they head to the next round. As Bernard summed up, 'This is just the beginning.'
