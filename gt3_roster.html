Driver Profiles

Audi Quattro Speedworks

Jason Davison, United States

#66, Audi Quattro Speedworks

Jason Davison is a 50-year-old American veteran of the racing world, known for his endurance, technical precision, and deep understanding of car mechanics. Jason has established himself as a versatile driver with a knack for mastering any machine. Off the track, Jason is passionate about designing custom car parts, which he prints using his 3D printer. His workshop is filled with unique prototypes, each a testament to his creativity and love for racing innovation.

Otto Reuter, Germany

#99, Audi Quattro Speedworks

German racer Otto Reuter has built a reputation for his consistency and team spirit in GT racing. With multiple wins in the ADAC GT Masters, he’s a dependable driver with a fanbase that stretches across Europe. Otto has a hidden talent: he’s a trained pianist and frequently plays at charity galas organized by his team.

Audi Sport Castrol Racing

Felix Wagner, Germany

#15, Audi Sport Castrol Racing

Felix Wagner, a veteran from Germany, has a celebrated racing career that spans Formula 1 and endurance racing. His legendary drive at the 24 Hours of Nürburgring secured his legacy in motorsport. Known for his wisdom on and off the track, Felix once built a vintage motorcycle from scratch, which he proudly rides to charity events.

Timo Klein, Germany

#16, Audi Sport Castrol Racing

Another seasoned German racer, Timo Klein has a list of victories across European endurance races, including several top finishes at the prestigious Nürburgring. His experience in both GT and DTM racing has made him a revered figure in the paddock. Timo’s unique passion? He’s an expert in viticulture and owns a small vineyard near the Rhine.

BMW M Power Michelin

Taylor Reece, South Africa

#31, BMW M Power Michelin

South African sensation Taylor Reece has made a name for himself in the DTM series, known for his rapid reflexes and strategic overtakes. His biggest accomplishment was clinching the DTM Rookie of the Year award. Taylor’s favorite pastime is surfing on South Africa’s wild coastlines, where he says he finds his balance.

Roberto Ferraro, Italy

#46, BMW M Power Michelin

Italian driver Roberto Ferraro transitioned from MotoGP to the world of GT racing. With a career adorned with seminal racing wins, Ferraro’s adaptability is legendary. Outside racing, he’s passionate about painting and often exhibits his abstract works in small galleries across Italy.

BMW Redline Elite

Nathan Hale, United Kingdom

#72, BMW Redline Elite

Young British talent Nathan Hale burst onto the scene with a record-breaking season in the British GT Championship. His precision driving and high-speed cornering abilities make him a strong contender. Nathan is also an amateur astronomer and loves stargazing on clear nights in the countryside.

Henry Ward, United Kingdom

#150, BMW Redline Elite

Known initially for his prowess in sim racing, British driver Henry Ward made a successful transition to real-world racing. His rise from the virtual to the physical track is a testament to his determination. Henry is a passionate advocate for mental health and frequently uses his platform to raise awareness.

Ferrari Competizione Nero

Jens Braun, Germany

#10, Ferrari Competizione Nero

German GT racer Jens Braun is a force on the track, known for his aggressive racing style. His top finish in the Nürburgring 24 Hours solidified his status. Jens enjoys cooking and has his own collection of international recipes.

Simon Ellis, United Kingdom

#69, Ferrari Competizione Nero

McLaren Velocity Racing

Alex Quinn, Switzerland

#59, McLaren Velocity Racing

A Swiss prodigy in motorsport, Alex Quinn quickly rose through the ranks of European touring car circuits. Known for his precise driving style, he claimed the junior championship title in his debut season. Alex’s career highlights include a spectacular comeback victory at the Nürburgring. Outside of racing, he enjoys paragliding over the Swiss Alps, where he says he finds the focus he brings to the track.

Yuki Yamamoto, Japan

#95, McLaren Velocity Racing

Hailing from Japan, Yuki Yamamoto is known for his calm under pressure and has a steady track record in the GT circuits of Asia. With multiple podium finishes in his local championships, Yamamoto has shown resilience and tenacity. One interesting fact: Yuki is the first cousin (his father’s brother’s son) of recent World Series Champion Yoshinobu Yamamoto.
