Race Recap: Week 1
Canadian Tire Motorsports Park

Opening Drama and Intense Battles

The inaugural race of the IMSA GT3 World Championship season at the storied Canadian Tire Motorsports Park unfolded with intense drama and thrilling battles. With 30 laps completed and three lead changes, the competitors left everything on the track, pushing their limits on the high-speed, technical corners of Mosport.

Practice & Qualifying Drama

The tone for the weekend was set early in the practice session. Jason Davison dominated, setting a quick pace, though he had a few brushes with the limits, incurring five track incidents. Martin Ragginger and Luca Stolz followed closely, hinting at a potential showdown in the main event.

Qualifying saw Kevin Estre edge out Ragginger for pole position with a blistering lap, securing his place at the front. Davison, despite showing early promise, would start fourth after Estre, Ragginger, and Klaus Bachler posted faster times.

The Race

As the race began, Estre and Ragginger quickly established themselves as the frontrunners, trading the lead in the opening laps with calculated moves and daring overtakes. Davison, starting fourth, stormed forward, capturing the lead by lap 3 and holding it tenaciously for an impressive 27 laps. But with just a few laps remaining, Estre made a brilliant comeback, capitalizing on a small miscalculation by Davison at the notorious Moss Corner.

With Estre reclaiming the lead, Ragginger seized the opportunity to chase him down, finishing a tight 0.9 seconds behind. Julien Andlauer, who had started fourth, steadily climbed through the ranks, showcasing consistent pace and skill to secure third place.

Final Standings

  • 1. Kevin Estre (Car #911) - 200 Points
  • 2. Martin Ragginger (Car #44) - 180 Points
  • 3. Julien Andlauer (Car #6) - 160 Points
  • 4. Klaus Bachler (Car #33) - 140 Points
  • 5. Jason Davison (Car #66) - 120 Points

Battles and Highlights

  • Davison’s Missed Opportunity: Leading much of the race, Davison’s minor mistake in Moss Corner cost him the top spot, a harsh reminder of the track’s unforgiving nature.
  • Late Surge by Andlauer: Known for endurance racing, Andlauer’s steady handling in the challenging corners secured his podium finish and valuable points.
  • Defensive Moves: Mirko Bortolotti charged up the field, facing challenges from Jack Aitken and Yelmer Buurman. Buurman held his position, finishing seventh.
  • Kirchhofer vs. Gamble: Teammates and rivals, Marvin Kirchhofer and Tom Gamble fought a fierce duel, with Kirchhofer finishing eighth and Gamble struggling with tire wear to 12th.

Key Takeaways

Kevin Estre’s victory at Mosport puts him in early contention for the championship, while Martin Ragginger’s strong showing suggests he’s not far behind. Jason Davison’s near-win will fuel his drive in upcoming races, and Andlauer’s podium shows he’s a steady competitor. Fans can expect fierce rivalries and surprises as the season continues toward the next race in Braselton, Georgia, at Road Atlanta.
