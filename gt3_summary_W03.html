Round 3 at Silverstone Circuit
**for immediate release**
April 20, 2024

Race Summary

Round 3 of the GT3 World Championship at the historic Silverstone Circuit marked the first endurance race of the season, challenging drivers to adapt their strategies for the extended race format. The combination of high-speed straights, technical corners, and increased fatigue tested both driver and machine over 42 grueling laps.

Qualifying Struggles for Davison, Bernard Shines

Thierry Bernard (#911) set the bar high in qualifying, clocking an impressive lap of 2:01.81 to claim pole position. Lukas Huber (#44) followed closely behind, with Johan Khumalo (#60) securing a solid third. Jason Davison, struggling with setup issues, managed only 10th place on the grid—a significant setback for a driver expected to contend for podiums.

Reflecting on his difficulties, Davison shared his frustration: “We couldn't get the setup right this weekend and struggled both in quali and the race. It just wasn't our weekend.”

Race Recap: Bernard Leads from the Front

From the start, Thierry Bernard (#911) took control, demonstrating exceptional endurance and precision as he led for 39 of the 42 laps. Huber made a valiant attempt to pressure Bernard, even seizing the lead briefly, but Bernard’s consistent pace allowed him to regain and maintain his position to the checkered flag.

“The track is fast and unforgiving, but today everything came together,” said Bernard. “It’s always special to win at a place like Silverstone.”

Midfield Action and Lefevre’s Climb

While Bernard dominated at the front, the midfield was a battleground. Pascal Lefevre (#6) showcased endurance race prowess, climbing from 7th to finish in third, overtaking Khumalo in the final laps. Sergio Conti (#19) also maintained a steady drive, securing fourth place in a disciplined race that rewarded consistency.

Finishing Order

  1. Thierry Bernard (#911) - 42 laps, 200 points
  2. Lukas Huber (#44) - +9.27 seconds, 180 points
  3. Pascal Lefevre (#6) - +28.06 seconds, 160 points
  4. Sergio Conti (#19) - +31.96 seconds, 140 points
  5. Johan Khumalo (#60) - +34.91 seconds, 120 points

Final Thoughts

Silverstone’s endurance format added a fresh dimension to the season, with drivers facing not only the technical demands of the track but also the test of stamina. Bernard’s victory underscores his adaptability, while Davison’s challenges reveal the delicate balance of setup and execution required in endurance racing. As the series progresses, the competition is heating up, with each race adding layers to the championship battle.
