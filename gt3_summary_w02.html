Round 2 at Road Atlanta
**for immediate release**
March 30, 2024

Race Summary

Week 2 of the 2024 IMSA GT3 World Championship took the drivers to the demanding 2.54-mile Road Atlanta circuit, a track that combines sweeping curves, technical esses, and high-speed straights that test the mettle of even the most skilled drivers.

Qualifying Set the Stage

Jason Davison (#66) clinched pole with a blistering lap of 1:20.99, once again proving his prowess. Hot on his heels was Thierry Bernard (#911), who qualified just a fraction behind with a time of 1:21.16. Markus Weber (#3) took third, showing a strong performance, while Lukas Huber (#44) and Pascal Lefevre (#6) rounded out the top five qualifiers. This set up a competitive front row, with all eyes on Davison and Bernard, two of the championship's strongest contenders.

Race Recap: Bernard Dominates in Dramatic Finish

The race began with Davison leading the charge. However, it was Thierry Bernard (#911) who showed his true colors on race day, seizing the lead on lap 8 and holding it for an impressive 22 laps. Davison struggled to reclaim his position but held a consistent pace, ensuring Bernard had to stay sharp. As the laps dwindled, Bernard pulled away, showcasing skill through Road Atlanta's notorious esses and high-speed turns. Davison managed to close the gap on a few occasions, but a minor error on Turn 12 in the final lap gave Bernard the space he needed to secure his victory.

“Road Atlanta is relentless. The pressure’s on you every second,” Bernard remarked. “I knew I had to stay focused with Davison right there.”

Battle in the Midfield

Meanwhile, Weber and Huber battled fiercely for third place, with Huber managing a daring overtake on the back straight but losing his edge through Turn 6. The two traded positions multiple times, but Huber ultimately clinched the spot, finishing a tight two seconds ahead of Weber.

“Every corner here feels like a battle,” Huber said after the race. “Markus and I had some great back-and-forth moments.”

Finishing Order

  1. Thierry Bernard (#911) - 31 laps, 200 points
  2. Jason Davison (#66) - +7.29 seconds, 180 points
  3. Lukas Huber (#44) - +21.40 seconds, 160 points
  4. Alex Quinn (#59) - +22.90 seconds, 140 points
  5. Pascal Lefevre (#6) - +25.64 seconds, 120 points

Final Thoughts

With two lead changes and zero cautions, Road Atlanta lived up to its reputation as a high-stakes, high-reward circuit. As the series progresses, drivers like Bernard and Davison have established themselves as fierce competitors. Bernard’s victory here shows that the championship battle is just heating up, with each driver aiming to take control of the leaderboard.
