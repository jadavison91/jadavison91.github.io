Race Report: GT3 World Championship - Round 5 at Autódromo José Carlos Pace

  • Location: São Paulo, Brazil
  • Track: Autódromo José Carlos Pace – Grand Prix
  • Weather: Clear skies, 78°F, light wind from the north at 2 mph
  • Date: May 15, 2024
  • Race Distance: 29 laps

Qualifying Recap

In qualifying, Jordan Lee (#08) showcased his dominance with a pole-winning lap of 94.22 seconds. Kai Becker (#188) was close behind with a time of 94.31 seconds, while Jason Davison (#66) secured third, proving his consistency yet again. Simon Ellis (#69) and Yuki Yamamoto (#95) rounded out the top five. The tight margins between the top drivers hinted at a thrilling race ahead.

Race Recap: Lee’s Commanding Victory Amidst Fierce Battles

From the moment the green flag waved, Jordan Lee maintained his composure and led the field with a flawless drive. Jason Davison made an aggressive start, overtaking Kai Becker in Turn 3 to move into second. Meanwhile, Simon Ellis and Yuki Yamamoto engaged in a fierce battle for fourth, with Ellis emerging ahead after a daring overtake through Bico de Pato.

The race’s drama intensified on Lap 15 when Roberto Ferraro capitalized on a slip by Yuki Yamamoto at Pinheirinho, claiming fifth place. Ferraro’s defensive prowess kept Yamamoto at bay for the remainder of the race. Further down the field, Andrea Hudson and Otto Reuter thrilled the fans with their side-by-side battle, which lasted for multiple laps.

As the race approached its conclusion, Kai Becker mounted a charge to close the gap to Lee, but a lockup at Descida do Lago dashed his hopes of victory. Jordan Lee crossed the finish line to claim his first win of the season, with Becker and Davison completing the podium.

Finishing Order

  1. Jordan Lee (#08) - 200 points
  2. Kai Becker (#188) - 180 points
  3. Jason Davison (#66) - 160 points
  4. Simon Ellis (#69) - 140 points
  5. Roberto Ferraro (#46) - 120 points

Final Thoughts

The Autódromo José Carlos Pace delivered yet another captivating race, showcasing the grit and skill of the GT3 drivers. Jordan Lee’s commanding victory was a testament to his pace and precision, while Kai Becker and Jason Davison solidified their positions as championship contenders. As the series moves to its next destination, the stakes continue to rise, promising even more thrilling battles.
